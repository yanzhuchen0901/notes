What reaches (造成的另一种表达) audience is 有血有肉的人（用否定增强气势）flesh-and-blood person with contradictions and imperfections but a carfully crafted public persona. Traditionally respected role models-such as scientists, educators, orthinkers-have never been admired because they lived flawless lives. They, too,were individuals with limitations, yet their achievements, ideas, and long-termdedication to society offered inspiration to others. More importantly, even amid thelimitations, setbacks, and imperfections of human nature,they were still able topersist in the pursuit of meaningful work.
Contemporary entertainment idols, by contrast, are often **elevated(这个好)** into symbols of total wxcellence. From appearance to talent, even personality and lifestyle, they are portrayed as if success **permeated(弥漫，散布)** every dimension of their lives. Even when such excellence is uneven or exaggerated, publicity systems quickly step in their image, while devoted **fans(狂热粉)** readily explain away the inconsistencies.

Once the (complexities of real life emerge)好强的表达 , the illusion of perfection rarely survives.Admiration, properly understood, is not anembrace of perfection, but a recognition that the essence of human life lies instriving within the boundaries of imperfection.
